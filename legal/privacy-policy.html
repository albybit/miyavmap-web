MiyavMap — Legal & Safety

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 July 2026

Overview

MiyavMap is a community app for photographing real street cats, collecting them as cards, and helping identify and look after them (the "Street Cat Society"). This policy explains what data the app collects, why, on what legal basis, and what rights you have over it — including the rights guaranteed by the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the UK GDPR.

MiyavMap has no advertising, no analytics or tracking SDKs, no cookies, and does not sell your data. There is no company server beyond Google Firebase, which processes data on our behalf as described below.

Who controls your data

MiyavMap is developed and operated by an independent developer (referred to here as "the developer", "we", "us"). The developer is the data controller for the personal data described in this policy. Questions, requests, or complaints about this policy can be raised using the contact details below — given the scale of this project, we are not required to and have not appointed a formal Data Protection Officer, but every request will still be answered by the developer directly.

What we collect, and why (with legal basis)

  • Anonymous session identifier — lets you browse the map before creating an account. Basis: legitimate interest in running the core service.
  • Email, display name, profile photo, if you sign in with Google — creates your MiyavMap account. Basis: performance of a contract (providing the account you're requesting).
  • Cat photos, capture time, approximate (fuzzed) location, nickname — the core "catch and collect" feature, publicly shown to other users. Basis: performance of a contract; you take an affirmative action (capturing a cat) to create each piece of public content.
  • Cat identity records, community updates, follows — the shared cat-identity and Street Cat Society features. Basis: performance of a contract, on the same footing as above.
  • Report text, reporting account, reported content — lets moderators review abuse reports. Basis: legitimate interest in keeping the platform safe and usable.
  • Approximate device location (never precise GPS — the app only requests coarse location, accurate to roughly 2 km), never stored as-is — computes location fuzzing and a city name, on your device only. Basis: legitimate interest, necessary to provide fuzzed locations at all.

We never use your data for advertising, profiling, or any automated decision that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you — the app's rarity/scoring system is gamification, not profiling in the GDPR sense.

What becomes public

MiyavMap's map is a shared, public database — every signed-in user (including anonymous ones) can see it. When you capture a cat, the photo, an approximate location, the capture time and your nickname are stored and shown to everyone. The same is true of cat profiles you help build: names, care notes and photos posted about a street cat are public and attributed to your nickname, as are your public stats and any helper roles you choose to display.

Location

MiyavMap never requests precise GPS access at all — it asks Android only for approximate (coarse) location, which the system delivers with roughly 2 km accuracy. That approximate position is used momentarily, on your device, to work out roughly how many other cats are nearby and to look up a city name. The coordinates actually saved to a card are additionally fuzzed — snapped to a grid of roughly 110 metres, with a small random offset — so your exact capture spot (often your home) is never collected in the first place, let alone published. Your device's precise location is never accessed, transmitted or stored by MiyavMap.

On-device photo checks

Every photo you take is checked for a cat using an on-device machine-learning model before it can be saved. This check runs entirely on your phone — the photo is never uploaded anywhere for it, and it works even offline.

Reports and moderation

If you or another user reports a card or a cat update, the report (its text, the reporting account and the content it refers to) is stored so it can be reviewed. Reports are only visible to MiyavMap's moderators, never to other users. We don't currently run reports through an automatic deletion schedule — they're retained until reviewed and kept afterward as a moderation record, rather than being deleted routinely. You can ask us to delete a report you filed by contacting us (see Contact, below); we'll honor that unless keeping it is necessary to investigate an ongoing safety issue.

International data transfers

MiyavMap's backend runs on Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore database and Cloud Storage), which may process and store data outside the European Economic Area, including in the United States. Google acts as our data processor and, under its own Cloud Data Processing Addendum, applies safeguards recognized under GDPR for these transfers (Standard Contractual Clauses and/or its EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework certification). The map itself uses OpenStreetMap tiles — loading a tile sends only an anonymous, standard tile request, with no account information attached.

Your rights (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If data protection law applies to you, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectify inaccurate data — most of it (nickname, avatar, roles) you can already edit yourself in the app; anything else, contact us.
  • Erase your data ("right to be forgotten") — the in-app "Delete account" (Settings > Account) does this immediately for your cards, photos and profile; see the note below on what stays. If you no longer have the app installed, see the Account & Data Deletion page.
  • Restrict or object to our processing of your data where it's based on legitimate interest.
  • Data portability — request a copy of your data in a structured, commonly-used format; the app doesn't yet have a self-serve export button, so contact us and we'll provide one manually.
  • Withdraw consent at any time, for any processing that relies on consent, without affecting the lawfulness of processing before the withdrawal.
  • Lodge a complaint with your country's data protection supervisory authority at any time — you don't have to contact us first.

To exercise any of these rights, use the Contact details below. We'll respond within the timeframes GDPR requires (generally one month).

Deleting your account

You can delete your account at any time from Settings. This permanently deletes your cards, your photos and your profile. Some community content that other users' collections depend on stays visible, with your name removed, as the deletion screen explains: votes you cast on others' cards, cat updates you posted, and cat profiles you were the first to register for others to follow. This reflects a genuine technical constraint (no cascading rewrite of every document your activity touched) rather than a business choice to retain your identity — your name specifically is removed from all of it.

If you have uninstalled the app, the Account & Data Deletion page explains how to request deletion without reinstalling.

Data retention

We keep your account data for as long as your account exists. If you delete your account, we delete what's described above immediately; the exceptions (votes, updates, registered cat profiles) are retained but stripped of anything identifying you. We don't currently run any additional time-based deletion schedule beyond that.

Children

In line with GDPR Article 8, MiyavMap is not directed at children under 16. If your country sets a lower digital-consent age (as low as 13, which some EU member states permit), that lower age applies to you instead. We don't knowingly collect data from anyone below the applicable age, and anyone found to be below it will have their account removed on request. See also our Child Safety Standards.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app changes. Material changes will be reflected here with a new "last updated" date; if a change meaningfully affects your rights, we'll surface it in the app.

Contact
