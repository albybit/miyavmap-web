MiyavMap — Legal & Safety

Child Safety Standards

Standards against child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) — for the MiyavMap Android app (package it.catchcat.app).

MiyavMap has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation. Child sexual abuse material (CSAM), grooming, sextortion, and any other form of child exploitation are strictly prohibited on MiyavMap, in any content or interaction the app offers.

How the app is designed to reduce risk

  • Not directed at children: MiyavMap requires users to be at least 16 years old (or the digital-consent age of their country, never below 13), and is not designed for or marketed to children.
  • Photos must contain a cat: every photo is checked by an on-device machine-learning model before it can be posted — the app is built around photographing street cats, not people.
  • No private messaging: there is no direct user-to-user chat; interactions are limited to public, cat-focused content (cards, cat profile updates and replies).
  • No precise locations: the app collects only approximate device location, and stored positions are further blurred before anyone can see them.

Reporting

Every piece of user-generated content in MiyavMap (cards, cat updates, replies) carries an in-app Report action. Reports go directly to the developer acting as moderator and are reviewed reactively. Users can also block any other user from every content surface.

CSAE concerns can also be reported from outside the app, at any time, by email to albybit31@gmail.com with the subject "MiyavMap child safety report". This mailbox is the app's designated child-safety point of contact and is monitored by the developer.

What happens when CSAE is found

  • The content is removed immediately (moderator takedown) and the account responsible is terminated.
  • Apparent CSAM and exploitation are reported to the relevant authorities — law enforcement and/or the appropriate child-protection hotline for the jurisdiction (such as NCMEC in the United States or the INHOPE network of hotlines in Europe) — as required by applicable law.
  • We cooperate with lawful requests from law enforcement relating to child-safety investigations.

Compliance

MiyavMap complies with applicable child sexual abuse and exploitation laws in the regions where it is distributed, and with Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy. These standards are reviewed whenever the app's features change in a way that affects user interaction.

Point of contact

Child-safety point of contact: the developer, reachable at albybit31@gmail.com.
